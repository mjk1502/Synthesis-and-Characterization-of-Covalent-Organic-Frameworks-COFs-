# Synthesis and Characterization of Covalent Organic Frameworks (COFs) for Post-Combustion CO₂ Capture

## Overview

This research project focuses on the synthesis and characterization of Covalent Organic Frameworks (COFs) for post-combustion carbon dioxide (CO₂) capture. The objective was to develop a porous, crystalline adsorbent using a greener solvent system and evaluate its suitability for industrial CO₂ separation.

The project was carried out under the guidance of **Dr. Sweta** at **IIT (BHU) Varanasi**.

---

## Objectives

- Synthesize porous COFs using a solvothermal method.
- Employ an environmentally friendly solvent system for improved sustainability.
- Characterize the synthesized material using FTIR, XRD, and BET techniques.
- Investigate CO₂ adsorption behavior and structure–property relationships.
- Study the influence of synthesis parameters on crystallinity, porosity, and adsorption performance.

---

## Materials

- 1,3,5-Tris(4-aminophenyl)benzene (TAPB)
- Terephthaldehyde (Ta)
- n-Butanol
- Deionized Water
- Glacial Acetic Acid
- Methanol
- Acetone
- Diethyl Ether

---

## Methodology

1. Preparation of precursor solution.
2. Solvothermal synthesis of TAPB-Ta COF.
3. Washing and activation of the synthesized material.
4. Characterization using:
   - FTIR Spectroscopy
   - XRD Analysis
   - BET Surface Area Analysis
5. Evaluation of adsorption properties.

---

## Characterization Techniques

### FTIR
- Confirmation of imine (C=N) bond formation.
- Analysis of functional groups.

### XRD
- Evaluation of crystallinity.
- Phase identification.

### BET
- Surface area measurement.
- Pore size distribution.

---

## Key Results

- Successfully synthesized porous TAPB-Ta COF.
- FTIR confirmed imine bond formation.
- High crystallinity and porous framework suitable for CO₂ adsorption.
- Studied the effect of synthesis parameters on material performance.
